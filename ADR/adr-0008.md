# ADR-0008: Game scene flow — a persona-routed hub world of role "buildings," gated by a defeatable gatekeeper, with a magic door to the canonical payoff

← [ADR index](../ADR.md)

[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) (places the challenge mechanic inside a scene structure; refines its granularity) ·
[ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) (realises the game half of the two-differentiator thesis) ·

**Drivers:** persona-specific discovery · memorability · product-judgment north star · honesty (§A/§E) ·

## Context

[ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) committed the site to a game as a co-differentiator; [ADR-0006](adr-0006.md)
built the engine-free entry scene up to the moment the visitor picks **play**; [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)
decided *what a challenge is* (a persona-framed predict-then-reveal evidence check, reveal-and-continue,
never a hard gate, magic-door shortcut always available). What none of them decided is the **shape of
the game itself** — the sequence of scenes a player moves through once they choose to play. That gap
must close before the engine can be chosen, because the scenes define what the runtime has to do, and
the engine is judged against *that*, not the reverse.

The scene flow proposed (Hasrul's own description):

1. **Introduction** — "Welcome, adventurer. What is your quest here?" → recruiter / founder-owner /
   casual adventurer.
2. **Begin the quest** — after persona + avatar are chosen, a welcoming NPC briefs the player on the
   quest.
3. **The overworld** — the avatar walks to NPCs or **buildings tied to a role** (Backend, Embedded,
   Scrum Master …); pressing *continue* enters that world.
4. **Inside a role-world** — the player learns Hasrul's background in that area, via text **and
   portfolio screenshots**. At the exit stands a gatekeeper ("Gandalf"). The player passes by
   completing a challenge.
5. **The finale** — the player meets Hasrul's own avatar ("professional Hasrul").

Two constraints from Hasrul sharpen scene 4 and resolve the obvious tension with
[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md):

- **There is no wall.** The gatekeeper is *theatre, not a lock*. The challenge is the
  [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) predict-then-reveal check: commit an answer, see the reveal, pass regardless
  (reveal-and-continue). A wrong or skipped answer still lets the player through.
- **The magic door is surfaced on entry.** When the player enters a role-world they are reminded they
  carry a **magic door** that can take them **directly to professional Hasrul, bypassing the
  gatekeeper** — the in-game manifestation of [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md)'s content-first escape hatch and
  [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)'s magic-door shortcut.

So this ADR does **not** supersede [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md): the no-wall + magic-door decision keeps its
core invariants intact. It does **refine one detail** — the unit a challenge attaches to (see Decision).

## Prior art

A game-structure decision; references are world/level-design and onboarding patterns.

**hajj.buzz — walkable stations gated by challenges to advance** (the model in
[ADR-0005](adr-0005.md)). *Borrowed:* the walk-up-to-a-place-then-complete-something loop, and the
two-mode shared identity. *Differs:* our places are **role-buildings chosen per persona**, the gate is
**defeatable theatre rather than a real lock**, and a magic door bypasses traversal entirely.

**RPG hub-world / town-with-buildings** (a central overworld whose buildings are sub-areas — Zelda
towns, the hub levels of many action-RPGs). *Borrowed:* buildings-as-topics navigation, so each
"building" is a self-contained chapter the player chooses to enter. *Differs:* our buildings map to
**role lenses** ([lenses.ts](../src/content/lenses.ts)), and which buildings are emphasised is driven
by the persona router, not free exploration alone.

**Boss-gate / "you shall not pass" gatekeeper** (Gandalf in *LOTR*; the boss at a dungeon's end).
*Borrowed:* the instantly-recognisable gatekeeper as a **memorable narrative beat** that marks the end
of a chapter and creates the commitment moment [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) relies on. **Explicitly not
borrowed — the lock itself:** the gatekeeper never blocks progress, never produces a fail/again state.
Souls-like "defeat to proceed" gating is rejected (it would break content-first and reverse
[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)).

**Museum / exhibition wing** (themed rooms, each exhibit = artifact + caption). *Borrowed:* the
"exhibit" pattern for showing **portfolio screenshots** — a real artifact with a short framed caption —
inside a role-world. *Differs:* gamified and persona-routed rather than a static gallery.

**Fast-travel / waypoint teleport** (open-world fast-travel; Doraemon's Anywhere Door, the original
"magic door" reference). *Borrowed:* instant teleport that frees the player from forced traversal.
*Differs:* ours teleports specifically to **the canonical payoff** (professional Hasrul / the readable
facts), binding the shortcut to the content-first guarantee rather than to mere convenience.

**What we did *not* borrow — and won't.** Hard boss gates / defeat-to-proceed lockouts; trivia-quiz
gates (named-rejected in [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) / [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)); forced linear traversal
with no fast-travel; and unskippable cutscenes (rejected already in [ADR-0006](adr-0006.md)).

## Decision

The game is a **persona-routed hub world**: a small overworld of **role "buildings,"** each a
self-contained chapter that teaches Hasrul's work in that role and ends with a *defeatable* gatekeeper
posing one [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) evidence check. A **magic door**, surfaced on entry to every world,
teleports the player straight to the finale (the canonical payoff), bypassing any gatekeeper.

**The five scenes:**

```
  ① WELCOME / QUEST SELECT      ── ADR-0006 welcome + role; sets ?persona=
        │  recruiter · founder/owner · casual
        ▼
  ② AVATAR + QUEST BRIEFING     ── ADR-0006 avatar step, then an in-world NPC
        │                          briefs the quest AND hands over the MAGIC DOOR
        ▼                          ("use it anytime to go straight to Hasrul")
  ③ THE HUB (overworld)         ── walk to NPCs / role-buildings; <continue> enters one.
        │                          Which buildings, and their order, is PERSONA-DRIVEN.
        ▼
  ④ INSIDE A ROLE-WORLD         ── learn the background here: text + portfolio screenshots
        │                          (exhibits). At the exit: the GATEKEEPER —
        │                          ┌─ poses the ADR-0007 predict-then-reveal check
        │                          ├─ commit → reveal → PASS REGARDLESS (no wall)
        │                          └─ MAGIC DOOR always bypasses him
        ▼   (return to hub, or magic-door to finale)
  ⑤ AUDIENCE WITH HASRUL        ── meet Hasrul's avatar ("professional Hasrul"):
                                    the synthesis + call-to-action. Magic door lands here.
```

**Geography = role lenses, not career stations.** A "building" is a **role lens**
([lenses.ts](../src/content/lenses.ts): Backend · Embedded/IoT · Full-stack · QA/Test ·
Scrum-Master/PM · Tech-Lead/EM · Product/FDE). Inside a building, the content is the **stations tagged
with that role** ([stations.ts](../src/content/stations.ts) `tags`) — e.g. the Backend building draws
on Transjakarta, the Kawan Lama framework, PINTAR, etc. This reuses the existing model wholesale: no
new facts, just a new spatial arrangement of the same `lens × station` data the readable site already
renders.

**Persona drives which buildings matter** (the [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) D1→D2 relationship):

- **Recruiter** — their chosen **role lens** is their primary building; it opens first and is
  highlighted. Other buildings remain explorable.
- **Founder/owner** — the "buildings" reframe as the **ownership story**: the three pure-PE projects +
  the role-cycling arc (today's *boss mode*), not a per-role split.
- **Casual** — the world foregrounds the **human/origin zone** (Warnet, Korea, the human story).

The world is one hub; *what is emphasised within it* is persona-filtered. Exactly how each persona's
emphasis is laid out spatially is an implementation detail, but the rule binds: the router chooses the
emphasis, the renderer draws it.

**The gatekeeper is theatre, never a wall.** At a building's exit the gatekeeper poses the
[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) check for that role-world. The player commits an answer; the reveal surfaces the
verified, role-relevant fact; the player **passes regardless of correctness** (reveal-and-continue). The
gatekeeper exists for the *commitment beat* that makes the reveal stick — not to lock content. The
magic door bypasses him at any time.

**The magic door = content-first, made in-world.** On entering any role-world the player is reminded
they carry a magic door that jumps **straight to professional Hasrul** (scene ⑤ / the canonical
readable facts), bypassing every gatekeeper. This is [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md)'s "skip to text" and
[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)'s magic-door shortcut, dramatised as an item. The owed `&station=`-style deep
link ([ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)) is its external-link equivalent.

**Refinement of [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) (granularity, not substance).** [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) framed
checks as "one per station." Here a check attaches to a **role-world (zone)**, which may aggregate
several stations' worth of evidence for that role. The mechanic is unchanged — persona-framed,
predict-then-reveal, reveal-and-continue, honesty-bound; only the *unit* the check attaches to shifts
from career-station to role-zone. [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) is **not edited** (append-only); this ADR
records the refinement.

**Honesty (§A/§E) binds the new surfaces.** Portfolio **screenshots are real artifacts only** — no
fabricated UI, no mocked-up "products" Hasrul didn't build; each carries an honest caption. The
gatekeeper's prompts/options/reveals obey [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)'s rules (no false-claim distractors).
§E gaps stay visible — a role-world whose honest story includes a stack-deficit shows the bridge, not a
hidden flaw.

**Reuses the content model; adds an asset dimension ([ADR-0003](adr-0003.md)).** The text is the
existing `lens × station` data. The one genuinely new content type is **screenshot assets** (image +
caption, associated to a project/station). This extends the content model with a media field; it adds
no new *claims*. Exact schema lands with the Phase-3 code.

## Alternatives considered

- **Hard boss gate (must defeat the gatekeeper to advance)** — adds stakes and a clean game-y win
  state, but withholds canonical facts, punishes the hurried recruiter, and reverses
  [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md). *Rejected — Hasrul explicitly chose no wall; breaks content-first.*
- **No gatekeeper at all (walk in, read, walk out)** — simplest, but loses the commitment beat that
  makes the reveal memorable (predict-then-reveal). *Rejected — gatekeeper-as-theatre keeps the beat
  without the cost.*
- **Geography by career station / timeline** instead of by role-building — but the readable site
  already renders the timeline ([Timeline.tsx](../src/components/Timeline.tsx)); the game differentiates
  by offering the *role* cut, which is also the recruiter's mental model. *Rejected as the primary axis*
  (the timeline view still exists on the readable side).
- **One linear hallway of rooms (no explorable hub)** — easier to build, but loses persona-driven
  emphasis and the sense of choosing your path. *Rejected — the hub is where the router's "what matters
  to you" becomes spatial.*
- **A single end-of-game challenge** instead of per-world checks — already rejected by
  [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) (detaches prediction from discovery). *Rejected — unchanged here.*
- **Avatar/persona choice inside the game rather than at the door** — would duplicate
  [ADR-0006](adr-0006.md)'s entry scene. *Rejected — the entry scene already owns scenes ①–② up to
  "enter the game."*

## Consequences

- **This defines the runtime the engine-choice ADR is judged against.** Required capabilities: a
  walkable avatar, a small hub with enterable buildings, NPC dialogue, an exhibit view (image +
  caption), a challenge overlay, and a teleport. All modest, top-down, non-physics — confirming
  [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)'s read that Phaser / Kaplay / a DOM-React tile renderer are *all* viable. The
  engine ADR is the next owed decision and can now proceed.
- **A screenshot/asset dimension is owed on the content model** ([ADR-0003](adr-0003.md)): image +
  caption associated to project/station, real artifacts only (§A). Schema lands with Phase-3 code.
- **Two recurring character art assets are owed:** the gatekeeper NPC and the quest-giver NPC, in the
  same flat-SVG spirit as the existing host avatar ([HasrulAvatar.tsx](../src/components/HasrulAvatar.tsx)).
- **`&station=` / zone deep-link** ([ADR-0007](adr-0007.md), owed) doubles as the external form of the
  magic door — any role-world is directly linkable.
- **Founder and casual personas need their own world framing** (ownership story; human/origin zone) —
  not just a relabelled set of role-buildings. **Deferred to [ADR-0009](adr-0009.md)** (Draft): how
  non-recruiter personas traverse the hub (chronological journey vs a panoramic "whole-landscape"
  overview vs the role-building cut). Flagged so the game isn't silently recruiter-only.
- **Scenes ①–② are already built** ([EntryScene.tsx](../src/components/EntryScene.tsx)); the game proper
  is scenes ③–⑤. The entry scene gains one addition: the quest-giver that hands over the magic door.
- **Skippable / content-first preserved** — the magic door is surfaced on entry to every world, the
  gatekeeper never blocks, and the readable site still holds every fact unconditionally.
- **Supersede trigger:** if Phase-4 analytics show the hub/gatekeeper structure lowers reach-the-content
  rate or adds no recall over plain reading, a future ADR may flatten the scene flow — recorded as a new
  ADR, never an in-place edit here.
