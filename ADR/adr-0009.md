## Context

[ADR-0008](adr-0008.md) decided the game's geography as a hub of **role "buildings"** and noted this
fits the **recruiter** mental model directly — a recruiter walks to the building for the role they're
hiring for. But the same role-by-role layout does not obviously serve the other two personas:

- **Founder / owner** wants the **end-to-end ownership story** — the three pure-PE projects + the
  13-year role-cycling arc (today's *boss mode*) — which cuts *across* roles rather than sitting inside
  any one of them. A per-role split may fragment exactly the story they came for.
- **Casual / adventurer** wants the **human / origin** — the Warnet, Korea, the person behind the work
  — which is a narrative arc, not a role catalogue.

So the open question: **how does each non-recruiter persona traverse the same hub world?** The router
already decides *what matters* per persona ([ADR-0005](adr-0005.md)); this ADR decides *how that is laid
out and walked* for founder and casual, without rebuilding the world per persona.

## Candidate directions (not yet chosen)

These are seeds to develop, not a decision:

1. **Chronological journey.** The avatar walks Hasrul's career in time order
   (Warnet/academia → Pariti → PLN → Ecomindo → TU/e+Thermo → Alltrons), the world unfolding as a path.
   *Fits casual* (origin-first storytelling) and gives the founder the role-cycling arc in its natural
   sequence. Risk: long for a hurried visitor; leans on the magic door to stay skippable.

2. **Panoramic overview — the "highest mountain."** The avatar climbs to a vantage point and sees the
   **whole landscape of Hasrul's world at once** — all stations/roles laid out — then dives into
   whatever draws them. *Fits the founder* (big-picture, "every hat at a glance") and gives casual a
   map to roam. Risk: more layout/art work; needs a coherent bird's-eye composition.

3. **Reuse the role-building cut** from [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md) for everyone, with persona-specific
   *emphasis/order* only. *Cheapest* (one geography), but risks fragmenting the founder's cross-role
   ownership story and under-serving the casual arc.

These are not exclusive: e.g. a panoramic overview could be the *hub*, with a chronological path as one
route through it, and role-buildings as another. The decision will likely pick a **primary** traversal
per persona and note where they share machinery.

## Prior art (preliminary — to be expanded when this is decided)

- **World-map / overworld with a vista** (the "climb to see the map" beat common in open-world games) —
  seeds direction 2.
- **Timeline / journey scrollytelling** (and this site's own [Timeline.tsx](../src/components/Timeline.tsx)
  on the readable side) — seeds direction 1; note the readable site already tells the chronological
  story, so the game version must add something (spatial, walkable) rather than duplicate it.
- **hajj.buzz station progression** ([ADR-0005](adr-0005.md)) — the baseline walkable-stations model
  either direction builds on.

## Decision

**Deferred.** To be chosen before the founder and casual worlds are built. [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md)'s
recruiter role-building cut is sufficient to begin the hub; this ADR is resolved in the same phase, not
before it.

## Consequences (once decided — placeholder)

- Will reuse the [ADR-0003](adr-0003.md) content model and the [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) router; any new
  layout adds a *rendering* arrangement, not new facts (§A).
- Will stay bound by [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) (content-first, skippable) and [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md)
  (no-wall gatekeeper, magic door to the canonical payoff).
- May inform the engine-choice ADR if a panoramic/overworld composition raises the runtime bar above the
  modest one [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md) established — to be checked when chosen.
