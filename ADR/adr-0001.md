# ADR-0001: Site type — a persona-routing, content-first / game-optional personal site

← [ADR index](../ADR.md)

**Status:** Accepted (2026-06-20)
**Drivers:** product-judgment north star · discoverability · ship-fast · no wasted work
**Feeds:** [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md)

## Context

Hasrul is pivoting toward Product Engineer / Forward Deployed Engineer roles. The market shape for
those roles rewards a candidate who can scope, build, ship, and understand users alone. The standard
proof is a **public product** — which Hasrul does not currently have.

The idea: make *himself* the product. A personal site where the **visitor picks a persona** and the
story of Hasrul adapts to them:

- 🎯 **Recruiter** → picks a role lens (backend / embedded-IoT / full-stack / QA-test / scrum-PM /
  tech-lead-EM / forward-deployed-product); the site filters and reframes to answer "is he fit for
  *this* role?"
- 🚀 **Founder / Owner** → "boss mode": end-to-end ownership beyond any single JD — the pure-PE
  projects plus the multi-year role-cycling arc.
- 🕵️ **Casual visitor** → the human: hobbies, games, social, origin story.

Research into the genre established two things:
1. Gamified/interactive personal sites are a proven format (Robby Leonardi's Mario resume, Bruno
   Simon's 3D driving portfolio, hajj.buzz's pixel journey, Salvatore Casalino's interactive resume,
   Peter Oravec's 8-bit neighbourhood, Henry Heffernan's 3D OS, Jesse Zhou's ramen shop).
2. **No portfolio in the wild routes content by visitor persona.** Lots of "tailor to your audience"
   *advice*; zero sites that let the visitor pick and reshape the story interactively.

So the differentiator is the **persona-router**, not the visuals. The visual archetype is a
replaceable skin over that router.

## Prior art

The "career-as-an-interactive-experience" format is well-established. Each reference below is followed
by a one-line note on what this project borrows and how our goal changes the picture. The point of the
list is honesty: this design is assembled from proven parts plus one genuinely missing piece.

**Robby Leonardi — Interactive Resume (Super-Mario side-scroller).**
[rleonardi.com/interactive-resume](http://www.rleonardi.com/interactive-resume/) — Awwwards Site of
the Day (2013), the canonical gamified resume; arrow/scroll through four "levels" that map to career
sections. *Borrowed:* the proof that a playful, game-framed resume can outperform a static one and
become memorable. *Differs:* it's a single linear experience for everyone — no adaptivity to who's
viewing.

**Bruno Simon — 3D driving portfolio.** [bruno-simon.com](https://bruno-simon.com) — the genre
ceiling; drive a car through a WebGL/Three.js world to discover projects. *Borrowed:* the ambition
bar and the "explore, don't scroll" instinct. *Differs:* it optimises for *engineering wow* (months of
3D work); this project optimises for *product judgment*, so 3D is explicitly deferred (see Alternatives).

**hajj.buzz (Pixel Hajj) — pixel journey + content/app split.** A pixel-art journey game that teaches
by walking an avatar through stations with NPCs, **with a separate readable content site and the game
at `/app`**. *Borrowed directly:* the station-by-station journey, NPC-delivered explanation, and —
critically — the **content-first / game-optional split** that this ADR adopts. *Differs:* hajj.buzz
teaches one fixed subject; we reshape the content per visitor persona.

**Salvatore Casalino — Interactive Resume.**
[salvatorecasalino.it/interactive-resume](https://salvatorecasalino.it/interactive-resume) — Awwwards
Honorable Mention; scroll a character through illustrated landscapes that reveal experience. *Borrowed:*
the calm, readable "career-as-journey" side-scroll as a viable low-cost render. *Differs:* still one
audience, one path.

**Peter Oravec — 8-bit neighbourhood portfolio.** [peteroravec.com](https://peteroravec.com) — an
arrow-key pixel neighbourhood used to find CV and projects; the closest live analog to the eventual
Phase-3 pixel-walker render. *Borrowed:* the top-down pixel-walker mechanic as the v1.0 game skin.

**Henry Heffernan — 3D OS desktop.** [os.henryheffernan.com](https://os.henryheffernan.com) — a
bootable fake operating system as a portfolio. *Borrowed:* the idea that the *navigation metaphor*
itself can be the product. *Noted, not adopted:* a "log in as a persona" OS metaphor was considered
as the routing skin and parked as a possible future render.

**Jesse Zhou — Ramen Shop 3D portfolio.**
[case study](https://jesse-zhou.medium.com/jesses-ramen-case-study-77bae77ab5f0) — an explorable 3D
scene. *Borrowed:* reinforces the "explorable place" instinct; *differs:* same 3D-cost caveat as Bruno
Simon.

**Open-source starting points.** [petejank/interactive-resume](https://github.com/petejank/interactive-resume)
(pixel art) and [dokson/interactive-resume](https://github.com/dokson/interactive-resume) (video-game
style, maintained into 2026) — forkable references for the Phase-3 game, so that phase doesn't start
from zero.

**Closest analog to the *routing* idea (different domain).** Personal portfolios don't do persona
routing, but B2B/SaaS marketing sites routinely segment by audience — role- or industry-based landing
pages ("for developers" / "for enterprises") that reshape the same product story per visitor. *Borrowed:*
the audience-segmented-entry concept, lifted from marketing into a personal site. *Differs:* SaaS sites
infer or A/B the segment; here the visitor **self-selects** the persona, and the segmentation is the
headline feature rather than a hidden optimisation.

**What we explicitly did *not* find — and why it's the point.** Across this survey (and targeted
searches for "recruiter/founder/visitor persona toggle" portfolios), **no personal/portfolio site lets
the visitor pick a persona and reshapes the career story interactively.** The abundant material is
*advice* to "tailor your portfolio to your audience," never a site that does it at runtime. That gap is
the novel contribution of this project: persona-routing as the primary mechanic, with everything above
as the proven scaffolding around it.

A second decision is bundled here: **must a visitor be able to skip the game?** The reference site
hajj.buzz separates a readable marketing/content site from the game (game lives at `/app`). Recruiters
in a hurry, search engines, ATS parsers, and screen readers all need a non-game path. Games are not
indexable; the readable route is what gets Hasrul *found*.

## Decision

Build a **persona-routing personal site** whose architecture is **content-first and game-optional**,
structured as three decoupled layers:

1. **Content** — career as structured data (stations → projects → skills) with per-persona framings.
   Persona-agnostic at storage, persona-filtered at render. *The durable asset.*
2. **Routing** — persona + role-lens selection, **deep-linkable** (`?persona=recruiter&lens=backend`),
   so each job application can link to its tailored entry point. *The differentiator.*
3. **Render** — the skin. Ships first as a clean readable site (the "normal website"), later gains an
   optional pixel-walker game at a dedicated route (`/play`). *Disposable / swappable.*

The **readable, persona-routed site is canonical and always available.** The game is an opt-in
enhancement that renders the *same* content from the *same* model — never a separate site, never forced.

Rejected as the starting point: a single fixed game archetype (e.g. Bruno-Simon 3D) and a plain
static CV site (see Alternatives).

## Alternatives considered

- **Plain static CV / portfolio site** — discoverable and cheap, but no differentiation; doesn't
  prove product judgment; doesn't stand out for PE/FDE roles. *Rejected — it's the thing everyone has.*
- **Single-archetype game, no persona-routing** (Bruno-Simon 3D, Mario side-scroll, pixel walker as
  the *whole* site) — high wow, but rewards graphics skill over product judgment, is expensive
  (months), is not indexable, and forces every visitor through one experience. *Rejected as the core;
  the pixel-walker survives as an optional render layer (Phase 3).*
- **Persona-router with no readable fallback** (game-only) — maximises novelty but loses recruiters,
  SEO, ATS, and accessibility. *Rejected — discoverability is a hard requirement.*
- **3D explorable scene as v1** (driving/room/ramen) — the wow ceiling, but very high build cost and
  the effort goes into the renderer, not the router. *Deferred to a possible v2 flex, never v1.*

## Consequences

- **The readable site is the foundation, not a fallback.** The "normal website" *is* what gets built
  first (roadmap Phases 0–2) and is fully valuable on its own. If the game is never finished, the site
  still works and still differentiates. This de-risks the whole project.
- **Deep-linkable personas become a job-hunt tool** from v0.1 — every application links to its lens.
- **Read-vs-play is purely a Render-layer choice**, persona is a Routing-layer choice; they can never
  drift apart because both consume one content model.
- **The game engine is out of scope until Phase 3** — pulling it in early would violate ship-fast.
- **CV-grade honesty rules apply to all site copy** (no inferred facts; name stack gaps with the
  transferable bridge; language levels stated honestly; personal logistics kept private; PE framing
  keeps both the pure-ownership projects *and* the role-cycling story).
- Choosing "content-first" commits to a stack that renders indexable HTML well — constrains
  [ADR-0002](adr-0002.md).
