# ADR-0006: Entry flow — a narrative persona-selection front door, branching to read-or-play

← [ADR index](../ADR.md)

**Status:** Accepted (2026-06-21)
**Extends:** [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) (amends its *front-door* arrangement; preserves every invariant) ·
[ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) (this is the first place the shared visual identity lands)
**Drivers:** product-judgment north star · memorability · ship-fast · no wasted work · honesty (§A)
**Feeds:** [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md) (Phase 2)

## Context

Phases 0–1 are effectively done: the readable, persona-routed site works, deep links restore state,
and the three persona views render real content. The open question is now about **the front door**.

[ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) shipped the front door as a plain persona selector and put the game at a
dedicated `/play` route, with the readable site as the canonical default. [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) then
promoted the game to a strategic centrepiece and required **one shared visual identity** across the
readable site and the game — but did not say *where* that shared identity first appears, or what a
visitor sees when they hit the bare root URL.

The proposal under consideration: make the bare-root landing an **in-world welcome scene**. Hasrul (as
a character) greets the visitor — the "adventurer" — and invites them to find out his story. The
visitor **self-selects their role** (recruiter / founder-owner / casual adventurer), and only then
chooses to **play the game** or **enter the readable site**.

This is attractive for three reasons:

1. **The front door is the single most-viewed screen.** It is the cheapest, highest-leverage place to
   establish the shared identity that [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) requires — every visitor sees it, both
   modes branch from it.
2. **In-world self-selection beats a form.** Framing persona choice as "choose your role, adventurer"
   makes the routing mechanic — the actual differentiator — feel native rather than like a settings
   page. The chooser *is* the product demo.
3. **It unifies the two modes at the point of entry** instead of bolting the game on at `/play`.

The risk is equally clear, and it is exactly the risk [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) was written to avoid:
**friction before content**. A recruiter with thirty seconds who hits a "welcome, adventurer" cutscene
and cannot get to the facts will bounce. ADR-0001 rejected a "persona-router with no readable
fallback"; a narrative gate that *delays* reaching content is the soft version of the same mistake.
This ADR exists to capture the flow **and** the guard-rails that keep it from regressing the invariant.

## Prior art

This is a UX/flow decision, so the references are entry-screen and onboarding patterns, not full sites.

**RPG character-creation / "choose your class" screens** (Diablo class select; the character-pick
screen at the start of many RPGs). *Borrowed:* the "decide who you are before you enter the world"
framing — persona maps to *class*, the avatar is the *character you pick to represent you* — which makes
self-selection feel like the start of an adventure rather than a form. *Differs:* in an RPG the choice
changes the game; here the **persona** changes the *content*, while the **avatar is cosmetic only** (see
Decision). *Explicitly not borrowed:* the genre's literal *"Are you a boy or a girl?"* prompt — the site
**never asks for gender**; the visitor simply picks an avatar, which incidentally implies one.

**hajj.buzz — two-mode site, shared identity** (already the model in [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) /
[ADR-0005](adr-0005.md)). *Borrowed:* the dual-mode structure and a single identity binding both modes.
*Extends it:* hajj.buzz splits content site and game by *route* (`/app`); we unify them behind **one
narrative chooser** at the root, so the read-or-play decision is made in-world, after the persona is
known.

**Audience-segmentation splash gates** on B2B/SaaS sites ("for developers / for enterprises"), surveyed
in [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) as the closest analog to the routing idea. *Borrowed:* the audience-segmented
entry. *Differs:* SaaS gates infer or A/B the segment; here the visitor self-selects and the
segmentation is the headline feature.

**"Skip intro" / "skip cutscene" convention** (cinematic games, intro videos). *Borrowed directly:* the
escape hatch that makes a narrative entry acceptable — the skip is a first-class control, not buried.

**What we did *not* borrow — and won't.**
- **Forced, unskippable cutscenes.** They optimise for spectacle over the visitor's goal — the wrong
  axis for a site whose north star is product judgment.
- **Avatar choices that fork content or voice.** Tempting in RPGs; rejected here under §A — the avatar
  is a sprite, not a claim about Hasrul.
- **A Phaser/engine-driven intro.** Pulling the game engine in for the front door would violate
  ship-fast and the "no engine before Phase 3" roadmap rule.

## Decision

Replace the bare-root front door with an **engine-free narrative entry scene** that performs persona
self-selection in-world and then branches to read-or-play. The scene is built in HTML/CSS/React as
Phase-2 *game-feel* — **not** in Phaser.

**The flow:**

```
bare root (no ?persona)
        │
        ▼
  ┌─────────────────────────────┐
  │  Welcome scene               │   Hasrul greets the "adventurer";
  │  (shared visual identity)    │   establishes the shared identity.
  └─────────────────────────────┘
        │  choose your role
        ▼
  recruiter  ·  founder/owner  ·  casual adventurer      ── sets ?persona=
        │
        ▼
  ┌──────────────┬──────────────────────┐
  │  Enter site  │        Play           │
  │  (readable,  │   pick your avatar    │   ← avatar chosen HERE, on the
  │   canonical) │   then enter the game │     play branch only
  └──────────────┴──────────────────────┘
```

This decision **amends the front-door arrangement of [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md)** (plain selector front
door + game at `/play`) but **reverses none of its invariants**. The following are inherited as
hard requirements and are the acceptance tests for the scene:

1. **Content-first / canonical readable site.** "Enter the readable site" is always present and is the
   default, fully-valuable path. The game is still opt-in; nothing forces it.
2. **Deep links bypass the intro entirely.** Any URL carrying `?persona=` (and/or `?lens=`) skips the
   welcome scene and lands **directly** on the target view — this is the ROADMAP's job-hunt superpower
   (`?persona=recruiter&lens=backend` from a CV). The narrative scene appears **only** on a bare root
   with no persona param. A returning visitor is not re-gated.
3. **Skip-to-text is ≤1 click** from the scene, as a first-class control — never buried, never delayed
   behind animation the visitor can't dismiss.
4. **Engine-free until Phase 3.** The scene is HTML/CSS/React. Phaser stays out (roadmap rule).

**Avatar is a cosmetic, game-only choice** and is selected **after** the read/play branch, on the play
path. The visitor *picks an avatar to represent them* — the site **never asks for gender**; the chosen
avatar simply implies one. The avatar is also **customisable along emoji-style axes** (e.g. skin tone),
in the same spirit as emoji presentation options — again purely cosmetic, asked of no one, inferred from
nothing. Rationale: the avatar is the walking sprite; it has no meaning on the readable site. Asking
readable-only visitors to pick one is friction with zero content payoff and shortens the path to content
for no reason. Avatar selection is therefore not part of the canonical read path. Under §A the avatar
never changes content or Hasrul's voice, and **no visitor attribute (gender, skin tone, anything) is
asked for or recorded as a claim** — it is presentation only.

**Routing state stays in the URL** as today (`?persona=`, `?lens=`); the scene is a Render-layer skin
over the unchanged Routing layer. The three-layer separation is what makes this safe — the content
model ([ADR-0003](adr-0003.md)) and the Phase-1 router are untouched.

**Deferred (not decided here):**
- *Where the recruiter role-lens is chosen* — in-scene vs. on the existing `RecruiterView`. Leaning
  toward keeping it on `RecruiterView` to avoid bloating the intro, but left open.
- *How avatar state is carried into the game* (URL param vs. local storage) — a Phase-3 concern.
- *The exact welcome copy* ("find out my secrets" etc.) — flavour, but still bound by §A: no invented
  persona, no claims that don't trace to `%HASRUL_PROFILE%`.

## Alternatives considered

- **Keep the plain persona selector as the front door** ([ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) as-is) — lowest
  friction and already built, but spends the highest-traffic screen on a form and gives the shared
  identity nowhere cheap to land. *Rejected as a missed opportunity: the entry is where identity is
  cheapest to establish.*
- **Make the game the only / forced entry** (drop or hide the readable path) — maximal novelty, but
  breaks content-first and the skip path. *Rejected — same grounds as [ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) and
  [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md).*
- **A full Phaser cutscene intro** — most "game-like," but pulls the engine in before Phase 3 and risks
  a heavy, slow front door. *Rejected — violates ship-fast and the no-engine-yet rule.*
- **Avatar chosen *before* the read/play branch** (as in the original proposal) — tidier as a single
  linear funnel, but taxes every readable-only visitor with an irrelevant choice. *Rejected — friction
  before content for no content value.*
- **Avatar that forks content or Hasrul's narration** — adds RPG flavour, but manufactures persona facts
  that don't exist. *Rejected under §A.*

## Consequences

- **Front-door render changes; routing and content do not.** This is a Phase-2 Render-layer change and
  honours the three-layer rule. No content-model or router rewrite.
- **The welcome scene becomes the anchor of the Phase-2 shared-identity work** — it is the first and
  most-seen surface both modes share, so [ADR-0005](adr-0005.md)'s palette/type/motifs decision should
  be made *here first*. [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md) Phase 2 is updated in the same pass to list the
  entry scene as the concrete first deliverable of the shared-identity job.
- **Deep-link bypass is now a hard test**, not a nicety: a CV link must never route a recruiter through
  the cutscene. This constrains the routing entry logic (show scene ⇔ bare root, no persona param).
- **A small amount of friction is added for bare-root visitors**, mitigated by (2) deep-link bypass and
  (3) one-click skip — and bare-root arrivals are mostly founders/casual/curious, not the time-pressed
  recruiter (who arrives via deep link).
- **The `/play` route survives** as the game's home; the front door simply also offers the play branch
  once a persona is known.
- **Supersede trigger:** if Phase-4 analytics show the entry scene increases bounce or lowers
  reach-the-content rate vs. the plain selector, a future ADR may revert the front door to the plain
  selector — recorded as a new ADR, never an in-place edit here.
