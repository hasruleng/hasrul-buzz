# ADR-0010: In-game journey metrics — a one-number discovery summary at the finale (plus an ambient pace gag)

← [ADR index](../ADR.md)

**Status:** Accepted (2026-06-21)
**Extends:** [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md) (instruments the hub-world journey) ·
[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) (feeds the discovery/fit-meter it foreshadowed) ·
[ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) (the fit-meter; product judgment over engineering-wow) ·
[ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) (content-first / skippable — stats must never punish skipping)
**Drivers:** "measure what matters" · persona-specific discovery · memorability · honesty (§A) · visitor
privacy · ship-fast (flavor is de-scopable)
**Feeds:** [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md) Phase 3 (player HUD) and Phase 4 (Hasrul's product analytics)

## Context

Hasrul believes in measurement ("you cannot manage what you cannot measure") and wants the game to
**track and show stats during the avatar's journey** — e.g. distance walked, a pace tracker (he's a
runner), stations visited, NPCs met, and other interesting metrics.

The instinct is right and on-brand: a site themed *the product is me*, built by a measurement-believer,
that measures its own visitors' journeys, is a fitting flex. But the north-star
([ADR-0001](adr-0001.md)) — *product judgment, not engineering-wow* — forces a distinction the raw
request blurs. The candidate metrics fall into two very different buckets:

- **Discovery metrics** — role-worlds visited, NPCs met, evidence checks engaged, roles explored, share
  of *this persona's* relevant content surfaced. These measure the thing the whole site exists for
  ([ADR-0005](adr-0005.md): persona-specific discovery) and can **feed the recruiter fit-meter**
  ([ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) already promised this).
- **Flavor metrics** — avatar distance, pace. Pure personality. Genuinely fun, but no discovery value;
  if engineering effort pools here it is exactly the "wow" the north star de-prioritises.

There is also a trap. A stats HUD invites **completionism** — "2/7 stations visited" can guilt a
hurried recruiter into walking the whole world, which directly contradicts the content-first / skippable
guarantee and the magic door ([ADR-0008](adr-0008.md)). A visitor who magic-doors straight to the facts
made the *right* call for them; the stats must celebrate what they explored, never shame what they
skipped.

Finally, two distinct audiences hide behind "track and show": the **visitor** (a diegetic HUD that
makes the journey feel rewarding) and **Hasrul** (aggregate product analytics — already
[ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md) Phase 4). This ADR is primarily about the **player-facing** metrics; it
notes where the same instrumentation feeds Hasrul's analytics, but keeps the audiences separate.

A note on the adage, in the spirit of the repo's honesty rules: *"you cannot manage what you cannot
measure"* is widely attributed to Peter Drucker, but the attribution is disputed and Drucker (and
W. E. Deming, who explicitly rejected the literal claim) both warned against over-measuring. We keep the
*useful* half — measure deliberately — and let the caution shape the design: measure what advances
discovery, not vanity.

## Prior art

A product/UX decision; references are measurement and game-HUD patterns.

**Fitness/running apps (Strava, Garmin) — distance, pace, splits, a celebratory run summary.** Hasrul
is a runner, so the run-summary aesthetic is a personal motif. *Borrowed:* the *celebratory,
personal* framing of self-measurement and the end-of-activity summary card. *Differs:* our "distance"
is symbolic (a walk through a career), not exercise; and there is **no comparison against other
people** — no leaderboard (wrong axis, consistent with [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)).

**RPG / game stat HUDs + end-of-level summaries** (steps taken, areas explored, NPCs talked to,
completion %). *Borrowed:* the ambient HUD and the end-of-journey summary that rewards exploration.
*Explicitly not borrowed:* completion **gates** and "100%" nagging — they would weaponise the stats
against the content-first guarantee.

**Privacy-first product analytics** (Plausible / PostHog-style, cookieless, aggregate). *Borrowed:* the
privacy-respecting posture for any data that reaches Hasrul — client-side, anonymous, no PII. *Not
borrowed:* third-party behavioural tracking, fingerprinting, ad-tech.

**Quantified-self dashboards.** *Borrowed:* the meta-conceit that a measurement-believer's site measures
its own experience — reinforcing *the product is me*. *Differs:* the visitor's journey data is the
visitor's; we surface it to *them*, not harvest it.

**What we did *not* borrow — and won't.** Leaderboards, competitive scoring, and timers (rejected in
[ADR-0007](adr-0007.md) — wrong axis, and timers punish the hurried); completion gates / achievement
nagging that pressures a visitor to "finish"; and any invasive analytics / PII / fingerprinting.

## Decision

**Over-measuring is itself the failure mode, so the rule is: track many events quietly, but *show* at
most two numbers — one that matters, one that charms.** "Track" and "show" are deliberately separated:
the instrumentation can record several events cheaply (to feed the fit-meter and analytics), but the
visitor is only ever *shown* one headline metric plus, optionally, one flavor metric.

1. **Shown headline — one discovery number (primary).** A single persona-framed measure of *how much of
   their story they discovered* (e.g. "you explored 4 of Hasrul's worlds" / a fit-meter fill for the
   recruiter). This is the one number that matters and the visible face of the **fit-meter**
   ([ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) / [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)). The richer event set (NPCs met, checks engaged,
   roles explored) is *tracked* to compute it and to feed Phase-4 analytics, but **not displayed** as a
   wall of stats.

2. **Shown flavor — one pace/distance gag (secondary, capped, de-scopable).** The runner motif: avatar
   "distance" or **pace**. It earns its single slot by *revealing a human fact* (Hasrul runs) for the
   casual persona, not as vanity. Cut first if it threatens a ship.

**When the numbers show (the realtime-vs-end decision):**

- **The discovery number is shown only at the end** — a summary card at the finale (scene ⑤,
  [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md), meeting "professional Hasrul"). It is **never** a live on-screen counter
  during play: a persistent "3/7" reads as a to-do list and silently pressures the visitor to "complete"
  the world — the exact completionism trap that fights content-first and the magic door. Celebration
  belongs at the end, not as a running tally.
- **The pace/distance gag may be ambient and live** — e.g. a quiet pace ticker or a route line that
  draws itself as the avatar walks. This is allowed in realtime precisely *because it implies no duty*
  and cannot read as failure; it is atmosphere, not a score. It also appears on the finale summary card.

**Hard rules (the acceptance tests):**

- **Celebrate, never nag.** The summary frames *what you explored* ("you met 4 of Hasrul's roles"),
  never *what you missed* ("2/7"). No completion bar; no gate; **no live discovery counter**.
- **Skipping is first-class.** Using the magic door or skipping to text must never lower a "score" in a
  way that reads as failure. A recruiter who teleports to the facts succeeded.
- **No competition.** No leaderboards, no timers, no scoring against other visitors (inherited from
  [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)).
- **Privacy.** Journey data is the visitor's; tracked **client-side**. Anything that reaches Hasrul's
  Phase-4 analytics is **aggregate and anonymous** — no PII, no fingerprinting.
- **§A honesty.** Journey metrics describe the *visitor*, not Hasrul, so they assert no career claim. But
  any metric that *implies* a claim about Hasrul (e.g. "skills mastered") must trace to verified facts in
  the content model — the same rule that binds every other surface.

**Two audiences, one instrumentation.** A single client-side journey tracker (zones entered, NPCs met,
checks engaged, steps) feeds *both* the player-facing summary (Phase 3) and Hasrul's aggregate analytics
(Phase 4). They stay distinct in presentation: the player sees their own run summary; Hasrul sees
anonymous aggregates.

## Alternatives considered

- **No metrics at all** — simplest, but forfeits an on-brand "measure everything" flex, a memorability
  hook, and the concrete fit-meter data source. *Rejected — the idea is good and aligned; it just needs
  discipline.*
- **Vanity stats only (distance/pace as the headline)** — fun, but engineering-wow with no discovery
  value and a straight path to completionism. *Rejected as the primary framing; kept as capped flavor.*
- **Full competitive gamification** (leaderboards, scores, timers) — maximal "game," wrong axis; punishes
  the hurried and reframes discovery as competition. *Rejected — consistent with [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md).*
- **Hasrul-only analytics, nothing shown to the visitor** — that is already [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md)
  Phase 4 and doesn't satisfy "show stats" to the player. *Kept as the complementary track, not a
  substitute.*
- **Achievement/completion gating tied to stats** — adds stakes, but manufactures a duty to finish and
  shames skipping. *Rejected — breaks content-first ([ADR-0001](adr-0001.md) / [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md)).*

## Consequences

- **A small client-side journey-tracker is owed in Phase 3** — events for zones entered, NPCs met, checks
  engaged, and step/distance count. Modest; well within the runtime bar [ADR-0008](adr-0008.md) set.
  Note the track-many / show-two split: the tracker records more than the visitor ever sees.
- **The fit-meter ([ADR-0005](adr-0005.md) / [ADR-0007](adr-0007.md)) gets its concrete feeder** — the
  tracked events compute the one shown discovery number; this ADR makes the data source real.
- **The finale scene ([ADR-0008](adr-0008.md) ⑤) gains a journey-summary card** — the home for the one
  discovery number and the runner-pace gag. This is the *only* place the discovery number appears; no
  live HUD counter during play.
- **The runner motif may inform the Phase-2 shared visual identity** ([ADR-0005](adr-0005.md)) — pace /
  distance / a route line are candidate visual through-lines, if a light touch.
- **Doubles as a Phase-4 analytics feeder** — the same instrumentation yields anonymous aggregates for
  Hasrul, satisfying [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md) Phase 4 without a second tracking system.
- **Privacy posture stated** — client-side, aggregate-only off-device, no PII; this constrains the
  Phase-4 analytics choice too.
- **Skippable / content-first preserved** — celebrate-don't-nag and no-completion-gate are explicit
  acceptance tests.
- **Supersede trigger:** if the HUD measurably distracts from discovery, encourages completionism, or
  adds no engagement (Phase-4 data), a future ADR may trim it to the fit-meter alone — recorded as a new
  ADR, never an in-place edit here.
